question: What is KYC?
answer: KYC (Know Your Customer) is a process banks use to verify the identity of customers.

question: What is a savings account?
answer: A savings account is a bank account that allows individuals to deposit money and earn interest.

question: What documents are required to open a bank account?
answer: Typically ID proof (Aadhaar/PAN), address proof, and passport-sized photos.

question: What is a current account?
answer: A current account is used by businesses for frequent transactions with no interest earnings.

question: What is an ATM?
answer: ATM (Automated Teller Machine) allows customers to withdraw cash and perform basic transactions.

question: What is a debit card?
answer: A debit card allows users to spend money directly from their bank account.

question: What is a credit card?
answer: A credit card allows users to borrow money up to a limit to make purchases.

question: What is interest rate?
answer: It is the percentage charged or earned on money over time.

question: What is a fixed deposit (FD)?
answer: An FD is an investment where money is locked for a fixed period at a higher interest rate.

question: What is a loan?
answer: A loan is borrowed money that must be repaid with interest.

question: What is net banking?
answer: Net banking allows customers to access banking services online.

question: What is UPI?
answer: UPI (Unified Payments Interface) enables instant money transfers between bank accounts.

question: What is IFSC code?
answer: IFSC (Indian Financial System Code) identifies bank branches for electronic transfers.

question: What is a cheque?
answer: A cheque is a written order directing a bank to pay a specific amount.

question: What is minimum balance?
answer: It is the least amount required to be maintained in an account to avoid penalties.
